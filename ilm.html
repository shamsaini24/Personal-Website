I began my first co-op term at Industrial Light & Magic (ILM) exactly one month after I had completed my first year at BCIT. If you don't know what ILM is, they are a major player in the visual effects industry, with an extensive list of innovations, inventions, and a list of movies that would make any film buff gasp in awe. In short: they're the biggest company people have never heard of, but almost everyone has seen what they've done.

The job was mainly focused on supporting the artists that worked there with technical issues and requests, comparable to on site IT support. Additionally, I was assigned the responsibility to build and deploy workstations for new artists, on a weekly basis. Much of this work was assigned and tracked using a ticketing system, which was my first exposure to such a system. Learning how to effectively keep track of tickets and write them helped me immensely later on when it became a major part of my next co-op. The job did not involve much in the way of programming experience, but it did make up for it in other areas. The majority of the work was hardware related, which taught me great deal about the various components of commercial level hardware and their use. My interactions with the artists that I was assisting helped me improve my verbal communication skills, as I often needed to explain the problem to them in a simple way to them to convince why a certain actions were required to solve the issue. At the end of my three month contract I left the company, but I will never forget that experience.

Skills Required:

  • Computer hardware knowledge
  • Unix and Windows command line
  • Excellent prioritization ability

Skills Learned:

  • Various networking concepts
  • Exposure to commerical level hardware
  • Handling support tickets
